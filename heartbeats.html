Case study · Editorial & publication

Heartbeats

A 32-page annual publication for Island Health: the people, programs, and numbers behind a year of care in a public hospital district.

Client
Island Health
Role
Editorial design & layout
Year
2025
Scope
32-page publication · cover · infographics

Overview

Heartbeats is Island Health's annual publication, a 32-page magazine for the community that reports on the year in care: new providers, patient and staff stories, service lines, and the numbers behind them. I worked on it as part of Island Health's in-house marketing team, handling editorial design and layout across the issue.

The brief

A single issue has to carry a lot of different kinds of content (a cover feature, provider spotlights, first-person staff interviews, dense service statistics, and explanatory health pieces) and still read as one publication front to back. The job was twofold: give all that variety a consistent structure, and make clinical information approachable for a general audience.

A consistent structure

Features, profiles, and interviews share a common grid, type hierarchy, and color language, so a reader moving from a provider spotlight to a caregiver interview stays oriented. Pull quotes, section markers, and a repeated photography treatment tie the issue together.

Making the numbers legible

Some of the most important content is also the hardest to read as plain text: annual service volumes and screening outcomes. I translated those into infographics: a by-the-numbers spread that turns a year of service into a scannable set of figures, and a "Why Mammography Matters" visualization that explains screening with a hundred-person illustration instead of a paragraph of statistics.

Outcome

The issue holds together as one publication while giving each kind of story its own space, and the data-heavy sections read at a glance. The structure also gives the marketing team a base to build future issues on.
