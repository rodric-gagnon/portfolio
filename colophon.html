How this site was made

This portfolio was designed and built in a tight back-and-forth with an AI assistant. Here's the workflow — and where the human decisions stayed.

The short version

The site you're reading is itself an example of how I work with AI. I didn't hand-code it screen by screen — and I didn't hand the whole thing to a tool and accept whatever came back, either. I directed an AI assistant: generating real options to react to, then refining them out loud until the site was right. The design judgment stayed mine; the AI took care of production.

The workflow

It ran in the same clear phases I'd use on any design project — just much faster in the middle.

Who did what

The point of an AI-assisted workflow isn't that the AI does everything. It's the division of labor — the tool takes the production and the waiting, and I keep the direction.

Production

The AI handled

  • Generating palette, type, and layout options to choose from
  • Writing the HTML, CSS, and JavaScript
  • Optimizing and embedding every image, via a Python pipeline
  • Making edits the moment I called them
  • Keeping every page consistent as the system changed
Judgment

I decided

  • The brief, the audience, and the scope
  • Which direction was right — every palette, type, and layout call
  • The final system: warm paper, steel, and terracotta; Newsreader, Inter, Space Mono
  • The voice and wording of the copy
  • When something was done — and when it was off

Under the hood

Each page is a single, self-contained HTML file. Colors, type, and spacing are set once as CSS variables, so light and dark modes come from the same definitions. Every image is optimized and embedded directly in the file, so a page is fully portable on its own.

Try it: the toggle at the top-right flips the whole site between light and dark. Both themes read from the same variables — nothing is styled twice.

The point

AI collapsed the slow part — the production, and the wait between "what if" and "let me see it." I could look at real options in minutes and react, instead of building each one by hand. What makes the result work isn't the tool, though. The speed came from the AI; the direction, the taste, and the decisions didn't.
