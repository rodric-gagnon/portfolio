Focus
Brand systems, packaging & print

I design brand identities, packaging, and printed material for small businesses, craft producers, and nonprofits around the Pacific Northwest. A lot of my favorite projects start the same way. A maker has something good, and it needs to look the part: a distillery with a new spirit, an arts foundation finding its voice, a hospital foundation telling its year in a report.

What I do

My work runs from the mark outward (a logo, a color system, a set of type choices) and then into everything that has to carry it: bottles, business cards, posters, publications, and slide decks. I like the whole span, from the first rough sketch to the file that goes to the printer.

Brand identity & logo systems Packaging & label design Print & editorial Presentation design AI-assisted workflows

Work that has to be exact

Some of what I do lives under real constraints. Spirit labels, for instance, have to clear federal TTB review before they can go on a bottle: every mandatory element present, correctly placed and sized, without the design falling apart around it. I enjoy that kind of problem: making something that's expressive and exactly correct at the same time.

How I work

I work in clear phases (direction, exploration, selection, build) and I move quickly once a direction is set. I'd rather show options than describe them, get to a decision, and then execute cleanly. Increasingly that means pairing traditional print craft with AI-assisted workflows, which let me explore more directions and hand over more finished options in less time.

Beyond the studio

Alongside client work, I'm the Foundation Outreach Coordinator at WhidbeyHealth, helping tell the story of a rural health system and the community that keeps it going. It keeps me close to the mission-driven design I care about most.
